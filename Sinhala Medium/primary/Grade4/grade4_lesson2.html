Lesson 2: Addition - 1 - Let's Add Bigger Numbers!

For Grade 4 Students (Age 9-10) - Learn to add numbers up to 4 digits!

What We'll Learn Today:

You learned about addition in Grade 3. Now let's learn how to add even bigger numbers, especially when we need to "carry over"! In this lesson, you will learn:

  • To **add 3-digit numbers with carrying**.
  • To **add 4-digit numbers with carrying**.
  • How to correctly use **place values** when adding.

Adding 3-Digit Numbers with Carrying:

When we add numbers, if the sum in one place value is more than 9, we "carry over" the extra ten or hundred to the next place.

Example: 345 + 178

  • **Step 1: Add the Ones place.** $5 + 8 = 13$. 13 is 1 ten and 3 ones. Write 3 in the ones place and carry over 1 ten to the tens place.
  • **Step 2: Add the Tens place.** (Carried 1) $+ 4 + 7 = 12$. 12 is 1 hundred and 2 tens. Write 2 in the tens place and carry over 1 hundred to the hundreds place.
  • **Step 3: Add the Hundreds place.** (Carried 1) $+ 3 + 1 = 5$. Write 5 in the hundreds place.
  • **Answer: 523**
11 345 + 178
523

Adding 4-Digit Numbers with Carrying:

The same rules apply when adding 4-digit numbers. We start from the ones place and move forward, carrying over when necessary, all the way to the thousands place.

Example: 1234 + 5678

  • **Step 1: Ones place:** $4 + 8 = 12$. Write 2, carry over 1.
  • **Step 2: Tens place:** (Carried 1) $+ 3 + 7 = 11$. Write 1, carry over 1.
  • **Step 3: Hundreds place:** (Carried 1) $+ 2 + 6 = 9$. Write 9.
  • **Step 4: Thousands place:** $1 + 5 = 6$. Write 6.
  • **Answer: 6912**
11 1234 + 5678
6912
